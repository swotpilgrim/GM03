# DETAILED CHAPTER OUTLINES: The Roadside Revival (Part 1)

## Chapter 1: Breakdown at the Crossroads
**Target Word Count:** 1,700 words
**POV:** Ruth Morrison, third person limited
**Setting:** Texas crossroads town, Greyhound bus station and main street
**Time:** Early afternoon, first day of June 1980

**Purpose:** Introduce five strangers stranded together and establish the revival tent's dominating presence in an unnaturally quiet town.

**Beats:**
1. Bus transmission fails at desolate crossroads. Driver announces three-day repair wait. Five passengers disembark into 105-degree heat: Ruth (organized teacher), Dale (nervous with theology books), Shauna (practical nurse), Levon (ex-con, suspicious), Kimmy (young runaway, fake ID).
2. Group surveys town—five-block main street, truck stop diner, closed shops. Massive white revival tent dominates horizon. Ruth takes charge, suggests finding motel. Others bristle at her authority but follow.
3. Diner stop for information. Waitress mentions everyone's at Preacher Elijah Caine's revival. Town feels empty, eerie. Motel has vacancies (only guests are the stranded five). Kimmy stares longingly at tent. Levon wants nothing to do with church people.
4. Group disperses to rooms. Ruth organizes, Dale unpacks theology books nervously, Shauna assesses situation clinically, Levon scouts exits, Kimmy sketches the tent from her window. Strangers thrown together by circumstance.

**Dialogue Topics:** Complaints about heat and delay, introductions (names, vague destinations), waitress praising Elijah's miracles, Levon's cynical comments about cults, Ruth organizing motel arrangements, tension over who's in charge.

**Emotional Arc:** Initial irritation and suspicion between strangers shifts to wary cooperation. Ruth feels responsible despite not wanting leadership. Kimmy feels pull toward tent (longing for belonging). Levon is defensive and isolated.

**Key Details:** 1980 Greyhound Scenicruiser bus, payphone outside diner, jukebox playing Conway Twitty, revival tent visible from everywhere, dust coating surfaces, chrome diner with red vinyl booths, Ruth's spiral notebook, Dale's battered briefcase.

## Chapter 2: The Empty Town

**Setting:** Various locations around crossroads town

**Dialogue Topics:** Ruth's observations about closed institutions, Dale's concerns about abandoned church, Shauna describing pharmacist's odd behavior, Levon reporting no exit options, debate about attending revival, Kimmy's quiet request to see it.

**Emotional Arc:** Individual unease builds to collective concern. Ruth realizes something's systematically wrong. Dale's seminary training activates (recognizing cult patterns). Shauna's skepticism sharpens. Levon's street instincts sense danger. Kimmy's vulnerability shows.

**Key Details:** Dusty library with microfiche, shuttered Baptist church, pharmacy with 1980s medicine bottles, motel clerk's glazed expression, CB radio at truck stop, Ruth's methodical note-taking, heat that never breaks, crickets at dusk.
